**[[Stage 1 — Foundations]]** — Foundations (Week 1)** Get comfortable with the vault concept (a vault is just a folder of notes), Markdown syntax, and creating/linking notes with `[[ ]]`. Learn the command palette (`Ctrl/Cmd+P`) and quick switcher (`Ctrl/Cmd+O`). Don't over-organize yet — just write and link.

[[Stage 2 — Linking & Structure]] — Linking & Structure (Week 2–3)** Understand backlinks, the graph view, tags vs. links, and Maps of Content (MOCs) — hub notes that organize topics. This is where you decide your organizational philosophy: folders, tags, links, or a hybrid. Most people land on link-heavy with minimal folders.

[[Stage 3 — Core Plugins]] — Core Plugins** Obsidian ships with toggleable core plugins. The essential ones: Daily Notes, Templates, Backlinks, Outgoing Links, Tag Pane, and Search. Learn callouts and embedding (`![[note]]`).

[[Stage 4 — Community Plugins]] — Community Plugins** This is where Obsidian becomes truly powerful. Key ones to explore:

- **Dataview** — query your notes like a database
- **Templater** — advanced templating with scripting
- **Calendar** — visual daily-note navigation
- **Excalidraw** — hand-drawn diagrams
- **Tasks** — task management across notes

[[Stage 5 — Workflows & Methodology]] — Workflows & Methodology** Adopt a system: Zettelkasten (atomic linked notes), PARA (Projects/Areas/Resources/Archives), or your own hybrid. This matters more than any plugin.

[[Stage 6 — Advanced Automation]] — Advanced / Automation** Sync across devices (Obsidian Sync or git), publishing, and AI integration — which connects to your earlier Ollama and Claude/MCP work for querying your knowledge base with local or API-based LLMs.
